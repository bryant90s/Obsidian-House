Design a moody, minimal website for a digital archive project called “DEADLINK.”

DEADLINK documents abandoned websites, deleted pages, and forgotten corners of the early internet.

Tone:
Quiet. Mysterious. Minimal. Not playful. Not cyberpunk neon.

Visual Direction:

Off-black background (#111–#181818 range)

Soft gray typography

Muted blue or desaturated green accent

Editorial serif for headlines

Clean mono or grotesk for body

Generous negative space

No rounded bubbly UI

Subtle grain texture overlay

Homepage Structure:

Hero:
Large headline:
“The Internet Forgets. We Don’t.”
Small subtext explaining the archive.
Minimal CTA: “Browse the Archive”

Featured Entries:
Vertical card layout with:

Website name

Year archived

Short 2–3 line description

“View Snapshot” link

Timeline Section:
Horizontal scrolling timeline showing key internet eras:
1998 – Personal homepages
2004 – Flash microsites
2010 – Tumblr aesthetics
2015 – Early crypto landing pages

Archive Categories:

Defunct Startups

Lost Personal Blogs

Early Social Networks

Experimental Web Art

Search Section:
Minimal search interface with filtering by year and category.

About Section:
Short manifesto about digital memory.

Footer:
Simple. Structured. No heavy UI.

Requirements:

Desktop 1440px

Mobile layout with vertical emphasis

Strong typographic hierarchy

Editorial spacing rhythm

No SaaS components

No dashboard elements

Avoid:

Tech startup visuals

Bright gradients

Cartoon illustrations

Overly animated UI

Make it feel like a design-forward digital museum.
